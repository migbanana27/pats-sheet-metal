The Contractor's Guide to Spec-Perfect Ductwork

A 1-page checklist of the most common ductwork installation mistakes — and how custom fabrication locks out leaks, balances airflow, and trims energy bills for the life of the building.

Common Install Mistakes to Avoid
  • Unsealed or taped-only seams. Cloth & mastic tape fail within years. Specify sealed seams and mechanically locked joints.
  • Sharp, unradiused bends. 90° hard corners choke airflow and spike static pressure. Use long-radius elbows and gradual transitions.
  • Oversized or undersized ducts. Wrong CFM means noise, hot/cold spots, and wasted fan energy. Size to Manual D, not rule-of-thumb.
  • Excess flex run. Flex duct over 5′ straight, or any kinked run, cripples airflow. Keep flex short; hard-pipe the rest.
  • No turning vanes in mains. Trunk turns without vanes create turbulence and uneven branch feeds. Always vaned.
  • Skipping the pressure test. Never sign off without a leakage test. If you can't test it, you can't trust it.
